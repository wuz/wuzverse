I've been considering a new kind of TTRPG resource that we've been talking about a lot at
[Infinite Citadel](https://infinite-citadel.com).

I've taken to calling them **System Hacking Documents**: system agnostic, rule sets for adding specific kinds of
gameplay, lore, and more to your games.

## The basic idea

Modern game systems like Fifth Edition (5e) spend a lot of time codifying specifics into their rules sets — they define
what can happen, how it happens, and what the specifics around that happening do. Old-school systems (and revival games)
rely a lot more on table rulings over specific, codified rules — they give some examples of what can happen and how you
might rule that at your table and then let you loose to decide what to do at your table.

These systems work well, until you want to engage with a specific kind of subsystem in your game. Not every table wants
to play with the specifics of managing a stronghold or becoming traders, but when you **do** want to engage with that,
you generally have to hope there is some kind of homebrew or supplement that adds that flavor of gameplay for your
chosen system.

System Hacking Documents[^1] sit in-between full game systems and specialized homebrew/supplements. Instead of offering
rules for managing trade economies in D\&D 5e, a Trading SHD would specify a generic "mini-game" and guidance on how to
hook that in to the specific systems of Fifth Edition.

[^1]: Abbrievated SHD, like a System Resource Document is SRD.

## A few examples that already exist

I don't think this is a particularly new idea, but I don't know that it's ever been given an actual name. There are
actually a few different examples out there. My post on
[Fabula Ultima Research Rolls](/posts/fabula-ultima-research-rolls) could pretty easily be translated into an SHD.

Some of the BEST examples of SHDs I've seen are the amazing
[&&&&&&&&& Treasure](https://www.exaltedfuneral.com/products/treasure?variant=39609392889958) and
[Monsters &](https://www.exaltedfuneral.com/products/volume-2-monsters-pdf) books by Luke Gearing.

The treasure book has amazing text like this:

> AC as Leather. The wearer rolls damage dealt twice and takes the best result. Save once per week or become a bear for
> 1d6 days.

Quick, easy to apply to whatever game you're playing.

I've got a couple more ideas that I wanna write up, including a system-agnostic strongholds/followers hack and a trade
economy hack.
